Significant dates

1230: The Beginning of the Prussian Crusade

This year marks the formal start of the Prussian Crusade by the Teutonic Knights. Under the authority granted by the Holy Roman Emperor, the Knights began the conquest and Christianization of the native Baltic Prussian tribes. This action established the Monastic State (Ordensstaat) and created the territorial and political core that would eventually become Prussia.

1525: The Secularization of the State

This is the birth year of the Duchy of Prussia. The last Grand Master, Albert of Hohenzollern, converted to Lutheranism and secularized the Catholic Monastic State, dissolving the Teutonic Order. The territory became a hereditary, secular duchy, the first major state to officially adopt Protestantism, fundamentally changing its identity.

1660: Full Sovereignty Achieved

Through the Treaty of Oliva, Frederick William, the "Great Elector," secured the full and absolute sovereignty of the Duchy of Prussia from the Polish Crown. This act ended centuries of Polish feudal overlordship and finalized the consolidation of power in the hands of the Hohenzollern dynasty of Brandenburg-Prussia.

1701: Proclamation of the Kingdom

This year marks the elevation of the state from a duchy to the Kingdom of Prussia. Elector Frederick III crowned himself King Frederick I, an act that formalized the state's rise in status and power, setting the stage for its eventual recognition as a European Great Power.

1740: Accession of Frederick the Great

The accession of Frederick II "the Great" signaled the start of Prussia's true imperial ambitions. He immediately seized the wealthy province of Silesia from Austria, cementing Prussia's place as a dominant military power and establishing the famous Austro-Prussian rivalry for control of the German states.

1871: Unification of Germany

This is the culmination of Prussian history. Following the victory in the Franco-Prussian War, the German states were united, and the King of Prussia, Wilhelm I, was proclaimed German Emperor at Versailles. Prussia became the largest and most politically dominant state within the new German Empire.
