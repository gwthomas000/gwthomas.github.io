The History of Prussia

The Start of Prussia

Prussia started as a German state in 1525 and was ruled by the House of Hohenzollern until it became the Kingdom of Prussia in 1701. While the kingdom was powerful since its creation, it really started to gain power in the 18th century under Frederick the Great

The End of Prussia?

In 1871 Prussia united the other 25 states of Germany under the German Empire, yet Prussia did not relinquish any power. The Emperor of the German Empire was also the King of Prussia, and that was true until the end of the German Empire in 1918 after WWI. Prussia continued to exists until officially 1947 when the Allies made a decree to dissolve it, but was unofficially dissolved in 1932 when the German Chancellor transferred the powers of the Prussian government to himself

Key Events in Prussia

  1. Establishment of the Kingdom of Prussia (1701)

    The Hohenzollern dynasty, which ruled the German state of Brandenburg, was also the ruling family of the Duchy of Prussia. In 1701, Frederick I, a member of the Hohenzollern family, crowned himself King in Prussia. This act transformed the Duchy of Prussia into the Kingdom of Prussia and laid the groundwork for its rise as a major European power.

  2. Conquest of Silesia (1740-1748)

    Upon his ascension, Frederick II, known as Frederick the Great, inherited a well-trained army and a strong treasury. He used these assets to invade and conquer the resource-rich region of Silesia from Austria during the War of the Austrian Succession. This victory gave Prussia a significant economic boost and elevated its standing among other German states.

  3. Napoleonic Wars (early 1800s)

    Prussia suffered a devastating defeat at the hands of Napoleon in 1806, which resulted in the loss of a significant amount of territory. However, after Napoleon's later defeat, Prussia was able to regain its lost lands at the Congress of Vienna in 1815. This period spurred military and administrative reforms that would prove crucial later on.

  4. Unification of Germany (1871)

    Under the leadership of Prime Minister Otto von Bismarck, Prussia initiated a series of wars with Denmark, Austria, and France. These conflicts, known as the German Wars of Unification, led to the creation of the German Empire in 1871. Prussia became the dominant state within this new empire, and its king, Wilhelm I, was proclaimed the German Emperor. The unification of Germany effectively marked the culmination of Prussia's long history as a separate and influential state.

Important Dates in Prussia

  • 1525

    Albert of Hohenzollern, the last grand master of the Teutonic Order in Prussia, converts to Lutheranism and secularizes his fief, transforming it into the Duchy of Prussia.

  • 1740

    Frederick II, known as Frederick the Great, ascends to the throne and immediately invades Silesia, initiating the War of the Austrian Succession.

  • 1772

    Prussia participates in the First Partition of Poland, significantly expanding its territory and connecting its eastern and western lands.

  • 1806-1815

    Prussia faces defeat and occupation by Napoleon's forces during the Napoleonic Wars, leading to significant territorial losses, but later regains much of its territory at the Congress of Vienna in 1815.

  • 1862

    Otto von Bismarck is appointed Prime Minister of Prussia, beginning his efforts to unify Germany under Prussian leadership.

  • 1864

    Prussia, allied with Austria, wages the Second Schleswig War against Denmark.

  • 1866

    Prussia defeats Austria in the Austro-Prussian War (Seven Weeks' War), leading to the dissolution of the German Confederation and the formation of the North German Confederation under Prussian dominance.

  • 1870-1871

    Prussia leads the German states to victory in the Franco-Prussian War, culminating in the proclamation of the German Empire at Versailles on January 18, 1871, with the King of Prussia becoming the German Emperor.

  • 1918

    Following Germany's defeat in World War I, the monarchy is abolished, and the Kingdom of Prussia becomes the Free State of Prussia within the Weimar Republic.

  • 1947

    After World War II, the state of Prussia is formally abolished by the Allied Powers.
